Empower Framework Competitive Market Research
Executive summary
Empower’s Framework feature (MEDDPICC / BANT / SPICED + custom) is best understood as a methodology layer over conversations: it converts calls/meetings into a structured checklist + scoring system, and then carries that structure forward across repeat interactions with the same prospect/account (multi‑call “history”), including through Ask Empower V2 (per your internal product scope).

In March 2026 market reality, competitors implement “frameworks” in four main ways:

CRM-grounded deal boards + AI playbooks (strongest at deal-centric, CRM-connected methodology coverage): Gong’s AI Deal Reviewer assesses adherence to a sales methodology playbook; playbooks can be published into deal boards that combine AI insights with CRM fields. 
Conversation intelligence + pipeline tooling (strong at sales-only methodology and deal/pipeline workflows): Modjo’s AI Call Scoring is explicitly used to check performance vs a methodology and identify coaching opportunities; Modjo Deals is positioned to prioritise pipeline, highlight objections, and provide AI deal insights (CRM-connected). 
Telephony platforms with playbooks/scorecards overlays (strong at real-time guidance and QA scoring): Dialpad provides AI Playbooks with prebuilt methodology templates (including BANT/MEDDIC/SPICED) and tracks adherence in Coaching Hub; Aircall provides AI call scoring via customizable scorecards and automation rules (licence-gated). 
Meeting assistants / notetakers (strong at single-meeting extraction and templated summaries, usually not deal-centric): Fireflies provides “AI Apps” such as BANT and MEDDIC that extract methodology signals from meetings; Fathom provides customisable “Deal Summary” templates including SPICED, MEDDPICC and BANT, and markets SPICED-aligned summaries and multi-interaction monitoring. 
Empower Framework V1 can win immediately where buyers need (a) methodology discipline across calls + video meetings (not just Zoom-style meetings), (b) a structured checklist that sales managers and QA can trust, and (c) a strong compliance story—especially because you can analyse calls without requiring call recording (per your internal product direction). Your primary current gap versus Gong/Modjo is the same one you’ve already identified: Framework is not CRM-opportunity-grounded yet (no bi-directional CRM sync; CRM data not pulled), which limits how confidently you can pitch “deal health & forecasting” today. 

Empower Framework V1 overview
Empower Framework V1 (internal feature definition from you + UI screenshots provided) supports:

Out-of-the-box methodologies (MEDDPICC, BANT, SPICED) and customer-defined custom frameworks.
Opening strategy checkpoints (e.g., “welcome message”, “define objectives”), and per-metric checkpoints that customers configure. Your admin UI shows “opening strategy” + “add checkpoints”, and “Generate with AI” for checkpoint scaffolding.
Post-call framework scoring and feedback, with visible scoring per element (e.g., “Decision Criteria: Partially Fulfilled” with a score and checklist evidence), plus “what went well / what went wrong” blocks.
Multi-call history (V1 concept): after each call/meeting, users can see the framework result and view historic framework status across related calls for the same prospect/account across channels (calls, video, emails, etc.) (per your product design intent).

Availability: Framework is accessible inside Ask Empower V2, which makes it demo-friendly: reps/managers can ask for “MEDDPICC gaps” and “what to ask next,” instead of manually navigating recordings.

Current limitations (important for sales truth)
Framework V1 is multi-call, but it is not CRM-opportunity-grounded yet (not mapped to CRM deal objects), and there is no bi-directional CRM sync / CRM data pull into Empower (per your product constraints). This is the main reason you should not pitch Framework V1 as “forecasting” yet.

Roadmap direction (positioning)
Your intended next step—CRM-connected Framework—aligns with how Tier‑1/Tier‑2 leaders implement methodology: once an AI playbook is attached to an opportunity in CRM, you can use conversation evidence to drive deal health, risk, next best actions, and (eventually) forecasting. Gong explicitly frames this by combining deal boards with “important CRM data” and AI-driven insights, and by publishing playbooks into deal boards. 

How the market implements frameworks
Frameworks in this category usually answer one of two questions:

“Did we follow the methodology?” (call-level or interaction-level compliance/coaching)
“Is this deal qualified and progressing?” (deal-level qualification/forecast confidence)
Competitors map to these questions using different primitives:

Playbooks (real-time + post-call adherence)
Dialpad’s AI Playbooks are explicitly “step-by-step prompts” based on a chosen sales methodology, and Dialpad’s coaching dashboard shows adherence over time (skill improvement). 

Scorecards / call scoring templates (post-call scoring)
Modjo’s AI Call Scoring templates are designed to verify performance against a methodology and identify coaching opportunities; managers set templates/questions. 

Deal boards (CRM-connected deal-level views)
Gong’s deal boards combine AI insights with CRM-field context; Gong also provides AI Deal Reviewer which evaluates whether reps are sticking to a methodology playbook, explicitly to “better qualify your pipeline.” 

AI Apps / templates (meeting-level extraction)
Fireflies provides AI Apps for frameworks like BANT and MEDDIC—these apps extract methodology signals (e.g., Budget/Authority/Need/Timeline) from meeting content. 

Fathom provides deal summary templates such as SPICED/MEDDPICC/BANT and markets structured framework summaries across interactions. 

This matters because Empower Framework V1 should be positioned as “methodology evidence from conversations” today, while CRM-connected Framework is the path to “deal health & forecasting”.

Competitor deep dive focused on methodology tracking
Gong
Gong is the reference implementation for “frameworks become deal infrastructure”:

AI Deal Reviewer explicitly assesses whether reps are sticking to a sales methodology playbook to better qualify pipeline. 
Gong provides pre-built playbooks for common methodologies (MEDDICC, BANT, SPIN, Challenger, SPICED, Solution Selling, Sandler). These can be published into deal boards. 
Gong deal boards combine AI insights with CRM fields and provide a structured view of deal health. 
Implication vs Empower Framework V1: Gong is strongest where the buyer wants “methodology completion” tied to the CRM opportunity (deal board, pipeline, filtering by CRM fields). Empower’s V1 limitation (not CRM-grounded) is exactly the wedge Gong will use—so sales needs to proactively frame the roadmap without overpromising.

Modjo
Modjo is the most relevant EU mid-market competitor for “methodology as coaching + deal execution”:

Modjo’s AI Call Scoring templates are positioned for checking how well reps perform vs a methodology and identifying coaching opportunities, with explicit setup guidance around templates. 
Modjo also runs a CRM-connected pipeline layer: Modjo Deals is positioned to identify stalled deals, surface objections, and provide AI insights to help close. 
Modjo’s deal pipeline can integrate with CRMs like HubSpot/Dynamics to fetch deal pipelines and stages, making it deal- and stage-aware. 
Modjo also provides “Ask Anything about your deal” to query deal context from calls/emails. 
Implication vs Empower Framework V1: Modjo’s advantage is CRM-connected opportunity context and sales-only focus. Empower’s pitch is strongest when you lead with: (a) broader conversation coverage (calls + meetings; telephony-native), (b) less vendor sprawl, (c) Framework embedded inside Ask Empower, and (d) a compliance-friendly posture (including your no-recording analysis capability, per internal spec).

Dialpad, Aircall, CloudTalk, Kixie
These vendors are important because they sell into “framework-like” needs through playbooks and QA scoring, even if they are not full CI/RI platforms.

Dialpad

Dialpad’s AI Playbooks provide methodology-based prompts and Dialpad explicitly markets pre-built templates for BANT, MEDDIC, and SPICED (plus custom playbooks). 
Dialpad’s Coaching Hub and AI Scorecards support QA-style scoring and coaching analytics, with documents describing how Coaching Hub gives “real-time visibility” and a leaderboard. 
Aircall

Aircall’s Call Scoring allows managers to create custom scorecards with AI- and human-evaluated criteria; it also documents automated call scoring with automation rules and licensing requirements. 
Aircall’s AI product marketing states it can automatically score conversations on QA criteria and identify improvement opportunities at team and individual levels. 
CloudTalk

CloudTalk markets “AI Call Scoring” that evaluates performance across “skills, playbook alignment, and compliance — or any criteria you choose.” 
CloudTalk also markets conversation intelligence dashboards with talk/listen ratio, topic trends, sentiment analysis, etc. 
Kixie

Kixie positions “Live Call Coaching” as listen/whisper/barge functionality for real-time coaching. 
Implication vs Empower Framework: Telephony vendors can claim “we have playbooks/scorecards.” But they often lack (1) multi-channel meeting coverage, (2) cross-call methodology history across an account, and (3) the “Ask” layer that lets managers query gaps and evidence quickly. Dialpad is the strongest here because it combines playbooks + coaching hub + scorecards; Aircall and CloudTalk are increasingly strong in automated scoring but are more QA/contact-centre oriented than “deal methodology progression.”

Fireflies, Read.ai, Noota and basic notetakers
These tools are relevant because prospects may already have them, and their “framework” features are usually extraction templates, not deal-centric methodology infrastructure.

Fireflies

Fireflies offers a BANT Sales AI App that captures Budget/Authority/Need/Timeline signals. 
Fireflies also offers a MEDDIC Sales AI App for summarising meeting insights under MEDDIC framing. 
Read.ai

Read’s coaching is oriented around communication effectiveness (meeting coaching report, engagement/sentiment-type signals), not sales methodology completion (MEDDPICC/BANT). 
Noota

Noota emphasises templates and structured outputs; it supports “custom summaries/templates” and can transcribe already-recorded audio/video via upload, but public sources don’t clearly show first-class MEDDPICC/BANT/SPICED scoring as a product primitive (unspecified). 
Noota also markets “reminders that trigger during your meeting when a specific topic is discussed,” which is closer to agenda-driven checklists than deal methodology frameworks. 
Fathom and other note-takers

Fathom provides multiple “Deal Summary Templates” including SPICED / MEDDPICC / BANT and allows customisation. 
Fathom markets SPICED framework integration and monitoring SPICED elements across multiple interactions to assess deal health/progression. 
Implication vs Empower Framework: These tools can compete on “we can output a MEDDIC/BANT summary.” Empower should win when the buying requirement is: (a) standardised scoring/checklists with evidence, (b) manager-level visibility across many calls (not just one meeting), and (c) expansion to true deal-level infrastructure via CRM connection.

Sales battlecards for Framework
Versus Gong
Where Gong is strong
Gong is best-in-class at CRM‑connected deal boards that combine CRM fields with AI insights, and at methodology playbooks that can be published into deal boards. 

Silver bullets for Empower Framework V1
Empower wins when the buyer’s highest friction is tool sprawl and adoption, and when they need methodology discipline across calls + meetings in a telephony‑centric environment. For regulated orgs, lead with your internal differentiator: analysis without requiring recording (policy-friendly). (No public “Gong = no-recording analysis” claim is available; treat as comparison framing rather than a factual statement.)

Pitch line to win
“Start with methodology evidence from every conversation now — then connect it to CRM for deal health and forecasting as the next step.”

Demo path
SaaS Sales: Ask Empower → “Show MEDDPICC gaps across the last 5 touchpoints with Account X; what questions should we ask next?”
Insurance: “Framework compliance without recording” + evidence checklist for decision criteria/paper process.
Recruitment: Use custom framework (“candidate qualification”) mapped to checkpoints.

Versus Modjo
Where Modjo is strong
Modjo’s AI Call Scoring templates are explicitly designed to score calls against a methodology and surface coaching opportunities; Modjo Deals is deal-centric and CRM-connected. 

Silver bullets for Empower Framework V1
If the prospect uses Ringover or is telephony-heavy, lead with “one platform for calling + intelligence.” Then position Framework as a standardised methodology layer inside Ask Empower, and anchor to roadmap for CRM grounding.

Pitch line to win
“Modjo makes you build methodology templates; Empower gives you a methodology layer across every conversation — inside your communications stack.”

Landmines
Don’t claim “Modjo does not do deal intelligence”: Modjo Deals explicitly exists and is positioned to support pipeline reviews and deal insights. 

Versus Dialpad
Where Dialpad is strong
Dialpad AI Playbooks have prebuilt methodology templates (including BANT/MEDDIC/SPICED) and show adherence over time via Coaching Hub / dashboards. 

Silver bullets for Empower Framework V1
Dialpad’s strongest framing is real-time guidance. Your strongest wedge is multi-channel conversation intelligence + Ask Empower: “Ask for methodology gaps and evidence across all conversations,” plus your internal compliance differentiator (no-recording analysis).

Pitch line to win
“Dialpad guides a call; Empower proves methodology across the whole account journey.”

Versus Aircall and CloudTalk
Where they are strong
Aircall and CloudTalk are rapidly adding AI scoring: Aircall offers call scoring via custom scorecards and automated evaluation rules; CloudTalk markets scoring across skills/playbook alignment/compliance criteria. 

Silver bullets for Empower Framework V1
Win when the customer needs actual sales methodology structure (MEDDPICC/BANT/SPICED) rather than generic QA scoring, and when they need visibility across calls + meetings (voice-only platforms are easier to displace when video meetings matter).

Pitch line to win
“QA scorecards grade calls. Frameworks qualify deals. Empower does frameworks — and brings it into Ask Empower.”

Versus Fireflies and “basic note takers”
Where they are strong
Fireflies offers framework-aligned extraction through AI Apps like BANT and MEDDIC; Fathom offers deal summary templates for frameworks like SPICED/MEDDPICC/BANT. 

Silver bullets for Empower Framework V1
Win when the customer needs manager-grade, repeatable methodology scoring with evidence and history, not just a formatted summary. Your strongest path is “from notes to operational methodology tracking,” with Ask Empower as the interface.

Pitch line to win
“They summarise frameworks. Empower operationalises them.”

Versus Noota, Read.ai, Kixie
Where they are strong
Read.ai is strong at meeting coaching and communication effectiveness, not sales methodology. 

Noota is strong at templates and structured meeting outputs and supports uploads, but explicit MEDDPICC/BANT/SPICED methodology scoring is unspecified in primary sources. 

Kixie provides live call coaching (listen/whisper/barge) and dialer workflows. 

Silver bullets for Empower Framework V1
Empower wins when the prospect wants methodology discipline + evidence, not communication coaching or manual live monitoring.

Pitch line to win

Demo prompts and Q&A for sales teams
These prompts are designed to create a “wow” moment while staying honest about limitations (not CRM-grounded yet).

Sales rep prompts
“Show the MEDDPICC summary for the last conversation with [Account] and highlight what’s missing.”
“Across the last 5 touchpoints with [Prospect], which MEDDPICC elements are still red, and what should I ask next?”
“In ‘Decision Criteria’, show the evidence in the call (timestamps/quotes) that supports the score.”
Manager prompts
“For my team’s last 30 demo calls, which MEDDPICC element is most often incomplete?”
“Compare two reps on ‘Opening Strategy’ completion rate and show examples of best openings.”
“Which accounts have repeated touchpoints but still show low ‘Decision Process’ and ‘Paper Process’ coverage?”
Insurance prompts
“Show ‘Call Flow & Structure’ checkpoints completion for regulated disclosures (custom framework) without needing recordings.” (Use only if your internal product allows; do not claim broadly in customer-facing copy unless confirmed in final release notes.)
“List the top recurring objections in claims or renewal calls and map them back to framework checkpoints.”
Recruitment prompts
“Use a ‘Candidate Qualification’ framework and show missing stakeholder alignment and next steps.”
“Show the history of framework scores across interviews with the same client role.”
“What it can’t do yet” prompts (teach sales to avoid)
“What is the deal stage in CRM and forecast confidence?”
“Predict close date / commit probability based on CRM.”
These are V2/V3 outcomes and require CRM grounding and bi-directional sync.
