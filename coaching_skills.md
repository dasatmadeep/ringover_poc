Empower Coaching and Skills One Pager
Positioning and executive summary
One-line positioning: Empower turns every customer conversation into measurable skill improvement—AI-defined skills, team trend visibility, and instant practice via Pitchroom—so managers can coach in weeks, not quarters.

Empower’s upcoming Coaching & Skills upgrade shifts Empower from “analytics you browse” to coaching actions you take. It introduces a Skills-based coaching engine (auto-defined skills, team benchmarks, coaching urgency, and action recommendations) embedded inside Ask Empower V2, plus Instant Pitchroom to practice real scenarios. This is designed to deliver visible, manager-grade value within 1–2 weeks of usage by automatically surfacing: top skill gaps, who needs coaching first, why they’re underperforming, and what to do next—without requiring customers to build complex scorecards or playbooks.

What’s new
Empower Coaching & Skills is built on three components that work together:

Skills layer (auto-defined): Empower evaluates agents against a consistent set of skills (e.g., discovery depth, objection handling, next steps control, clarity, rapport) and tracks progress over time. This is fundamentally different from “manual scorecard projects” where teams must define questions and scoring before they can coach at scale.

Coaching Insights in Analytics: A dedicated Coaching section inside Analytics makes it easy to see skill distribution, team trends, and individual coaching urgency (so managers know where to spend time first).

Instant Pitchroom: One-click creation of practice scenarios/AI avatars based on real gaps (e.g., repeated pricing objections, missing stakeholder discovery), turning insights into practice—fast.

How Empower delivers value in the first two weeks
Empower’s promise is fast time-to-value, aligned to how managers actually coach:

Week one: baseline and prioritisation
Within the first week of normal usage, managers can see early patterns: which skills are weakest across the team, which reps trend up/down, and a short list of “coach first” candidates (coaching urgency). This “action first” approach is a direct contrast to competitors that depend on template/playbook authoring before meaningful coaching data exists.

Week two: repeatable coaching motions
By week two, managers can run a simple cycle:

identify top skill gaps (team + individuals),
select 3–5 calls to review (evidence-driven),
assign practice sessions via Pitchroom,
re-check skill trendlines the next week.
This is positioned to produce an early SWOT-style view of the team: strengths to replicate, gaps to address, risks to mitigate, and coaching opportunities.

Market landscape and competitive SWOT
The coaching market in 2026 clusters into four coaching models:

Enterprise CI/RI coaching (Gong): Gong scorecards provide structured feedback, and AI Call Reviewer can suggest answers or score calls automatically—reducing manual scoring time. 
 Gong also offers AI Trainer, an AI-powered roleplay environment based on real Gong-captured interactions. 

Strengths: mature enablement, enterprise workflows, roleplay training. 

Weaknesses vs Empower: heavier admin setup and platform complexity; typically evaluated in broader revenue-intelligence buying cycles (harder to adopt “just for coaching”).

Mid-market CI coaching (Modjo): Modjo’s “AI call scoring” is designed to verify team performance against a methodology and identify coaching opportunities, but it relies on templates managers create/set up. 

Strengths: methodology-based scoring, sales-focused workflows, solid EU presence. 

Weaknesses vs Empower: template-led setup (coaching value often follows configuration); narrower focus (sales-only positioning).

Telephony AI overlays (Dialpad, Aircall, CloudTalk):
Dialpad’s AI Playbooks provide templated playbooks selected by admins and deliver real-time insights plus post-call analytics for coaching opportunities. 
 Aircall markets AI call scoring and live guidance features as part of its AI suite. 
 CloudTalk markets call scoring across skills, playbook alignment and compliance criteria. 

Strengths: real-time assistance, voice-first QA/monitoring. 

Weaknesses vs Empower: coaching is often an add-on layer to telephony rather than a skills progression system with manager-grade trend visibility.

Meeting assistants with coaching elements (Read.ai, Fireflies, Fathom, Noota, Leexi):
Read.ai coaching focuses on communication metrics (pace, filler words, interruptions, questions asked) and provides real-time meeting metrics like sentiment/engagement and talk-time distribution. 
 Fireflies offers conversation intelligence (speakers/topics/sentiment) and Live Assist for real-time notes and suggestions. 
 Fathom includes a Coaching tab with AI Scorecards and behavioural metrics like talk time and monologues. 
 Noota’s pricing signals “Sales Coaching Tab” and “Interview Scorecard.” 
 Leexi’s pricing lists “Structured feedback/scorecard” in its top tier. 

Strengths: simple adoption, meeting-centric workflows, often lower price points. 

Weaknesses vs Empower: coaching tends to be meeting-centric or communication-centric, not a dedicated skills improvement engine across the full conversation stack.

How Empower wins
Empower’s coaching positioning should be anchored on four differentiators:

Skills-first coaching, not scorecard projects. Many competitors organise coaching around scorecards/templates/playbooks that teams must configure (Modjo templates; Gong scorecards; Dialpad playbooks). 
 Empower’s message: skills are auto-defined; coaching starts immediately.

Closed loop: insight → practice. Gong’s AI Trainer is the closest analogue to Pitchroom, but Empower’s differentiator is speed-to-action and integration into the same coaching flow. 

Multi-vertical coaching: sales + recruitment + support (most CI tools are sales-led; telephony QA tools are support-led). Use skill language that maps to each vertical, not “sales-only methodology compliance.”

Compliance-friendly coaching stance: Empower can analyse calls without requiring call recording (internal product capability)—a strong insurance/compliance wedge (position carefully and align to customer policy requirements).
