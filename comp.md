Strategic competitor research for Noota.ai, Leexi, and Fathom
Market positioning changes that matter for Ask Empower V2 selling
The “AI notetaker” space has matured into two distinct buying motions: tools bought by individuals for note-taking, and tools bought by managers for repeatable performance improvement. Your sales team will win more consistently if they treat Fathom as “sales-ops friendly notetaker + light CI surfaces” (deal view, coaching), while treating Fireflies as a “horizontal meeting database + broad automation” platform—similar outcomes (notes), different buyer expectations and competitiveness.

Fathom has moved beyond “single-meeting notes” by adding CRM-connected deal surfaces and coaching/behavioural metrics. Its help centre documents a Deals/Deal View experience (available when connected to HubSpot or Salesforce) that centralises call insights and links them to CRM fields. 
 Fathom also documents a Coaching tab with AI Scorecards and behavioural metrics like talk time and monologues. 
 This is why many buyers evaluate it closer to “light conversation intelligence” than classic notetakers.

By contrast, Fireflies positions itself as a meeting intelligence layer with (a) an AI assistant (“AskFred”) that can generate summaries and follow-ups from meeting content, and (b) a “Conversation Intelligence” suite focused on speakers/topics/sentiment and trend comparisons. 
 Fireflies also emphasises CRM automations like syncing notes/summaries/transcripts to HubSpot objects. 

For Noota and Leexi, the strategic point is that both vendors are pushing “EU-first, secure, integrated note-taking” narratives—often with VoIP + CRM + ATS as part of the pitch—so the way you win is less about “we also summarise” and more about multi-channel conversation coverage, org-wide intelligence, and telephony-native workflow.

Noota.ai
Noota positions itself as an AI copilot that turns meetings and calls into structured outputs, with strong emphasis on integrations—especially CRM and ATS use cases. Its integrations page explicitly states connections to CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot) and ATS (e.g., Bullhorn), plus productivity tools (Notion, Slack), and claims EU-hosted encrypted data with GDPR, SOC2, and ISO 27001 alignment. 

Noota’s pricing page provides unusually detailed hints about product direction: it lists Ask Noota, “automatically record meetings,” “global search,” mobile apps (iOS/Android), and a “Call Recorder (VoIP)” feature, plus deeper “Business” tier capabilities like custom templates and unlimited integrations. 
 It also signals expansion towards revenue-like and talent workflows through “Sales Coaching tab” and “Interview Scorecard” in the AI feature list. 
 Noota’s recruitment-oriented product pages reinforce that it sees hiring as a core vertical, with “automated ATS” positioning including structured interview reports and scorecard-like evaluation. 

What makes Noota strategically “different” from generic notetakers is the breadth of workflow claims: “VoIP call recorder”, ATS integrations, and structured report automation rather than only meeting summaries. 

Leexi
Leexi positions itself as an AI notetaker designed to connect meetings/calls into existing business stacks. Its integrations directory showcases broad coverage across conferencing, calendar, CRM, ATS, VoIP, collaboration, automation—and explicitly lists Ringover and Aircall among VoIP integrations, as well as CRMs like HubSpot and Salesforce. 

Leexi’s own pricing page highlights a “European and secure AI-notetaker” posture, stating data storage and transit “within France,” end-to-end encryption, GDPR compliance, and ISO 27001 certification. 
 Importantly for competitive threat assessment, the pricing table also suggests an up-market feature roadmap: “Structured feedback/scorecard,” “Automated alerts,” “Detailed analysis,” “Analytics on topics,” and “Customised reports” appear as tiered features. 

Leexi also promotes physical meeting capture: it has a specific in-person meeting use-case page advertising a mobile app for recording, transcription and analysis of physical meetings. 
 On the Zoom marketplace, Leexi is described as gathering a company’s calls & meeting recordings “together with your CRM data,” linking recordings to deals & customers—language that aims squarely at sales leaders, not only individual users. 

Strategically, Leexi is a frequent “Ringover adjacency” competitor: because it already integrates with Ringover, buyers can stay on Ringover telephony and procure Leexi for notes/analytics. 
 Your win condition is to show why Empower + Ask Empower V2 removes the need for a second tool while increasing team-level intelligence.

Fathom
Fathom’s homepage positions the product as a meeting assistant that syncs meeting notes/insights/action items into tools like Slack, Salesforce, HubSpot, Notion and Asana, and highlights “Ask Fathom” as a way to search meeting content. 

Where Fathom becomes strategically closer to “conversation intelligence” is the combination of CRM-connected deal surfaces and coaching features:

Fathom documents a Salesforce integration where call summaries can automatically sync/attach to matching Contacts, Accounts and open Opportunities after a meeting ends (with manual vs automatic sync options). 
 Its HubSpot integration includes “HubSpot Cards” that show the last five meetings on Company/Contact/Deal records inside HubSpot, with recording links and quick summaries. 

Fathom’s Deal View is explicitly designed for reviewing “sales deals and call insights,” with direct linking to HubSpot/Salesforce fields and automatic logging of notes/summaries/action items in the CRM record. 
 It also supports sales-methodology styled summary templates: Fathom documents deal summaries using templates such as SPICED, MEDDPICC and BANT. 

On coaching, Fathom documents a Coaching tab that includes AI Scorecards and behavioural metrics; AI Scorecards are positioned as team-admin managed and behavioural metrics include talk time, questions, monologues, etc. 
 Fathom also documents “Real-time coaching” notifications (talk time percentage and monologue detection). 
 It supports keyword alerts that notify users after meetings when keywords are mentioned (with options like “external attendees only”). 

Constraints are also important for competitive handling. Fathom states it only records Zoom/Teams/Google Meet calls if the user is present, which affects manager-led “always-on” capture expectations. 
 Fathom also states it does not offer real-time transcription; summaries/transcripts appear after the meeting is processed. 
 It additionally states it does not support uploading external recordings; it can only transcribe meetings it records live. 

For security-oriented buyers, Fathom’s help centre claims HIPAA, SOC2 Type II, and GDPR compliance, and states its AI sub-processors are contractually prohibited from training on user data. 

This combination—CRM-connected deal context + scorecards + behavioural coaching—is what makes Fathom feel closer to Modjo-style “sales conversation tooling” than horizontal note-only apps.

Practical segmentation for Sales
To avoid putting Fireflies and Fathom “in the same bucket,” treat competitor mentions as signals of what the buyer actually cares about:

If they mention Fathom, they usually care about sales workflow tightness with HubSpot/Salesforce and deal-level rollups, because Fathom explicitly sells Deal View and CRM-side surfacing of meetings. 
 They may also care about coaching metrics/scorecards if they’re on a team plan. 

If they mention Fireflies, they usually care about broad meeting capture and productivity automation plus meeting analytics. Fireflies markets AskFred as a meeting-content assistant and positions “Conversation Intelligence” as a way to analyse speakers/topics/sentiment and compare trends over time. 
 CRM hygiene is often part of the value story (e.g., syncing notes/summaries/transcripts to HubSpot objects). 

If they mention Noota, look for recruiting/ATS workflows and compliance-heavy documentation. Noota highlights ATS integrations (e.g., Bullhorn, SmartRecruiters, Recruitee) and recruitment-specific automation, alongside CRM integrations. 

If they mention Leexi, the conversation is often about EU security posture + VoIP/CRM integrations and “meeting/call analytics,” since Leexi actively markets France-hosted data, GDPR/ISO 27001, and integration breadth including VoIP vendors (Ringover, Aircall). 

Sales battlecards and Q&A by segment
When a prospect says “We already use Fathom”
What this usually means
They are likely getting value from a strong “notes → CRM” workflow (HubSpot/Salesforce) and/or they are starting to operationalise coaching via scorecards/behavioural metrics. 

Discovery questions to ask
Ask whether they’re using Deal View or CRM cards and what they expect from those surfaces, because Fathom’s deal tooling is explicitly gated by being on a team Business plan and connecting HubSpot/Salesforce. 
 Ask what coaching workflow they actually run: Fathom’s coaching features vary by role and plan. 
 Ask how important it is to capture conversations beyond Zoom/Meet/Teams, since Fathom states it only records those platforms and cannot ingest uploaded external calls. 

Win message to use
Position Empower (and Ask Empower V2) as the next step when they need intelligence not only for meetings, but across the full “calls + meetings” world and across the organisation—especially if telephony is central to their motion (prospecting, inbound support, screening calls). Keep the conversation anchored on coverage and adoption: Fathom requires the user to be present to record, which can limit “always captured” org visibility in some setups. 

Honest landmines
Do not try to win by claiming “Fathom is just a notetaker.” Fathom has explicit Deal View, CRM sync workflows, coaching scorecards, behavioural metrics, alerts, and real-time coaching notifications. 

When a prospect says “We already use Fireflies”
What this usually means
They want broad meeting capture and a searchable database with AI Q&A and analytics. Fireflies positions AskFred as an embedded AI teammate for meeting content, and it markets conversation intelligence tied to topics and sentiment trends. 
 They also likely care about CRM hygiene, because Fireflies pushes meeting notes/summaries/transcripts into HubSpot objects to reduce manual entry. 

Discovery questions to ask
Ask whether they use AskFred for cross-meeting questions or mostly per-meeting summaries; Fireflies documents both meeting-level and cross-meeting usage patterns. 
 Ask whether “meeting intelligence” is enough or whether they need structured intelligence across inbound/outbound phone calls too (this is where telephony-native coverage becomes the wedge). Keep CRM questions factual: Fireflies explicitly markets automatic HubSpot sync for meeting notes/summaries/transcripts. 

Win message to use
Position Ask Empower V2 as company-wide conversational intelligence purpose-built for customer-facing teams (sales/support/recruitment), not just a meeting database. Then double down on the “single system” story if they are already on Ringover for telephony (or want to be), because using Fireflies typically means one more vendor layer to manage (meeting bot + data governance + exports) while still relying on a separate phone platform.

Avoid saying
Do not claim Fireflies has no team analytics; it explicitly positions a conversation intelligence suite to compare trends and measure conversation quality over time. 
 Instead, differentiate on scope (calls + meetings) and on how Ask Empower V2 answers operational questions rooted in your customer conversation stack.

When a prospect says “We use Noota”
What this usually means
They care about (a) structured documentation, (b) automation into ATS/CRM, and (c) EU-hosted security posture. Noota’s help centre lists both CRM and ATS integrations, and its product messaging heavily targets recruiting workflows. 
 Noota’s own positioning also claims encrypted EU data storage and compliance alignment (GDPR, SOC2, ISO 27001). 

Discovery questions to ask
Ask which workflow is primary: sales, recruitment, support. Noota is unusually ATS-forward compared with many notetakers, so you want to know whether ATS automation is the “why.” 
 Ask whether they also need telephony capture or only meetings—Noota markets a “Call Recorder (VoIP)” on its pricing/features list, which may indicate they’re trying to expand beyond meetings. 
 Ask what outputs matter (scorecards, structured reports, follow-up emails), because Noota explicitly markets both “Interview Scorecard” and follow-up automation. 

Win message to use
If they are recruitment-led: position Empower’s value around multi-channel capture (calls + meetings), repeatable coaching, and operational visibility for managers—especially where phone calls drive throughput. If they are sales-led: anchor on telephony-native capture and Ask Empower V2’s company-wide intelligence (patterns, blockers, objections), and treat ATS-like scorecard features as “table stakes” rather than the differentiator.

When a prospect says “We use Leexi”
What this usually means
They chose an EU security-forward tool positioned around integrations and meeting/call organisation. Leexi highlights France-based data storage/transit, end-to-end encryption, GDPR and ISO 27001 compliance. 
 They also value connectivity: Leexi’s integrations list includes Ringover, Aircall, HubSpot and Salesforce among others. 

Discovery questions to ask
Ask explicitly: “Are you using Leexi mainly for note-taking, for CRM automation, or for analytics/scorecards?” because Leexi’s pricing page suggests structured feedback/scorecards and analytics live on higher tiers. 
 Ask which channel matters most: Leexi supports in-person meetings via mobile app, which is a strong point for field-heavy teams. 
 Ask whether they currently use Ringover (many do) because Leexi’s Ringover integration indicates a common “two-vendor” stack: Ringover for telephony, Leexi for intelligence. 

Win message to use
Position Empower as a consolidation and scale story: reduce stack complexity, keep data governance in one place, and move beyond “after-call notes” into Ask Empower V2’s organisation-wide conversational intelligence. In Ringover accounts specifically, the most persuasive argument is often operational: if they already pay for telephony, adding Empower gives them intelligence without another integration surface to maintain.

Vertical application cues for Sales, Recruitment, Insurance
For recruitment teams, Noota and Leexi will often win on “structured documentation + ATS integration.” Noota explicitly lists ATS integrations (Bullhorn, SmartRecruiters, Recruitee and others) and sells automated ATS workflows. 
 Leexi positions itself similarly via integration breadth, including ATS and VoIP categories, and supports in-person meetings via mobile recording. 

For sales leaders, Fathom is the most “Modjo-adjacent” of the notetakers in this set because it brings CRM-connected deal surfaces (Deal View) and coaching/scorecard capabilities that are legible to heads of sales and enablement. 
 In these deals, your strongest differentiators are (a) breadth of conversation capture (especially phone calls), and (b) whether the buyer wants a standalone meeting assistant or an intelligence layer connected to telephony and (soon) deeper revenue workflows.

For insurance (and other regulated environments), expect heightened scrutiny on data security, consent and hosting. Leexi emphasises France-based data storage/transit with GDPR/ISO 27001 messaging. 
 Fathom claims HIPAA, SOC2 Type II and GDPR compliance and provides consent-related controls and guidance. 
 Noota claims EU data centres plus GDPR/SOC2/ISO 27001 alignment. 
 The sales win is to be crisp on governance (where data is stored, how consent works, retention controls) and then tie it back to business outcomes (coaching, quality, customer risk signals) rather than “AI magic.”
